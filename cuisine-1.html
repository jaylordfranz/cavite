Bacalao de Cavite

Recipe Overview

Bacalao is a salted fish dish with vegetables that I grew up eating every Good Friday in my hometown, Cavite City. It is one of my comfort foods.

This dish reminds me of my childhood and gives me the Semana Santa feels. Bacalao is best served with steamed white rice or with hot pandesal.

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Dried Salted Cod Fish (or similar) 1/2 kilo
Extra Virgin Olive Oil 1/4 – 1/2 cup
Atsuete Seeds (dry) 2-4 tbsp
Garlic (minced) 4 cloves
Onion (chopped) 1 medium
Potato (cubed) 1 big
Garbanzos (cooked and peeled) 150 – 200 gms
Water 1/4 cup
Cabbage (cut into small pieces) 1 small
Red Bell Pepper (diced) 1 big
Patis and freshly ground pepper to taste

Procedure

  1. In a pan, heat the olive oil, fry the cubed potatoes until light brown or half cooked, remove from oil, set aside.
  2. In the same pan with the same oil, fry the atsuete seeds until color is released into the oil. Remove atsuete seeds, discard.
  3. In the same pan and oil, sauté garlic and onions until onions are limp and translucent; be careful not to burn the garlic.
  4. Place the shredded fish and sauté for about a minute.
  5. Add the garbanzos, mix well.
  6. Pour in the water, cover, and simmer for about 5 minutes.
  7. Add the cabbage, mix, and cook for about 3 minutes.
  8. Put in the semi-cooked potato and cubed bell peppers, mix well. You may add extra olive oil if needed. Season with patis and pepper to taste. Cover and cook for another 5 minutes.
  9. Serve hot with rice or pandesal. Enjoy!

Tradition

It is not only a dish but more of a tradition that has been passed on from generation to generation. Hoping that the next generations would love it as much as we do.
